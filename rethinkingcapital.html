Title :

Save the Holy River, Ganga!!

The Ganga is the largest river in India with an extraordinary religious importance for Hindus. Situated along its banks are some of the world's oldest inhabited cities like Varanasi and Patna. It provides water to about 40% of India's population across 11 states, serving an estimated population of 500 million people or more, which is larger than any other river in the world.

Yet our national river remains one of the most polluted in the world, denying vast populations of their rights to water, and robbing the world of the beauty and sanctity of a river that is worshipped by one billion people as divinity itself.

From past, many of initiatives have been undertaken to clean the river but failed to deliver desired results. After getting elected, India's Prime minister Narendra Modi affirmed to work for cleaning the river and controlling Pollution. Subsequently, Namami Ganga project was announced by the Government in July 2014 budget.

However, the problems are numerous, they are not insurmountable. As the problems have been created by humanity, they thus can be remedied by humanity, if the will and focus is there. Some of the major causes are increase in population density, meagre dry season water flows in the river due to upstream uses, enhanced per capita pollutants discharged to the river which includes sewage and municipal waste, industrial effluent and agricultural runoff.

Suggest your exceptional and innovative solutions to save the Holy River, Ganga.
